Case Study · TaskFlow

Designing a task manager that doesn’t get in your way

Problem

Most task apps felt heavy for simple daily planning. I wanted something that loaded fast, worked offline, and let users stay in the keyboard as much as possible.

Approach

I focused on a minimal layout: one list, clear priorities, and keyboard commands for all core actions. I used semantic HTML and ARIA labels to ensure it was usable with screen readers.

Outcome

The result was a small but fast app that users described as “calm” and “predictable.” Page weight stayed low and Lighthouse scores remained high across devices.

Case Study · FocusNote

Communicating a product with just one page

Problem

The goal was to clearly explain the value of a minimal writing app to non-technical visitors in just a few scrolls.

Approach

I structured the page around three questions: What is it? Who is it for? Why is it different? I used a tight typographic scale and consistent spacing to lead the eye down the page.

Outcome

The layout translated well to mobile, with clear headings and tap targets. This project helped me practice thinking like both a developer and a communicator.
